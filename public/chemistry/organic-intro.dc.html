// L3 — ALKANES: THE SATURATED FAMILY
Alkanes are the simplest hydrocarbons — carbon chains with every possible bond to hydrogen filled ("saturated"). Their general formula is CₙH₂ₙ₊₂. Methane, the gas you cook with, is the one-carbon alkane; the rest of a candle, petrol, and crude oil are longer chains from the same family.
// L3 — ALKENES: THE REACTIVE FAMILY
Alkenes have one C=C double bond, so they're "unsaturated" — missing some hydrogens compared to alkanes (CₙH₂ₙ). That double bond is a weak spot other chemicals can attack, which makes alkenes far more reactive — and is exactly what lets us link thousands of them together into plastics like polyethylene.
