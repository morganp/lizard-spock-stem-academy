Reactions & word equations

A chemical reaction rearranges atoms into new substances — nothing is created or destroyed. A word equation is just a sentence for that swap. Build a few yourself below.

// L1 — SIGNS A REACTION HAPPENED
Bubbles with no shaking, a colour change, heat or light given off, a new smell, a solid appearing from two clear liquids — these are your clues that atoms have rearranged into something new.
// L2 — CONSERVATION OF MASS
Reactions never create or destroy atoms — they only rearrange them. That's why the mass of the reactants always equals the mass of the products. Word equations obey this too: every atom on the left of the arrow reappears on the right, just wearing a different partner.
