// THE FINGERPRINT REGION
In an infrared spectrum, the region below about 1500 cm⁻¹ is called the fingerprint region. It's a dense, messy set of dips that is unique to each compound — like a barcode. You don't try to assign every peak; instead you match the whole pattern against a database. The bands above 1500 cm⁻¹ (O–H, N–H, C–H, C=O) are the ones you read directly to spot functional groups.
