// L3 — COLLISION THEORY
For a reaction to happen, particles must collide with enough energy — the activation energy — and in the right orientation. More collisions, or harder collisions, means a faster rate.
Concentration ↑
More particles packed into the same space means collisions happen more often.
Temperature ↑
Particles move faster, so collisions are both more frequent and more energetic — this is the biggest lever of the three.
Surface area ↑
Crushing a solid into powder exposes far more of it to collide with — same mass, much faster reaction.
