Forces & elasticity

Pull a spring and it stretches; let go and it springs back. Hang more mass and it stretches proportionally more — that neat straight-line rule is Hooke's law. Load the spring, plot the graph, find the point where the rule finally breaks, and measure the energy stored.

HOOKE'S LAW
F = k · e
Force (N) = spring constant k (N/m) × extension e (m). Double the force, double the extension — as long as you stay below the limit of proportionality. A stiffer spring (bigger k) stretches less for the same force: a steeper line.
ELASTIC vs PLASTIC
Elastic (below the limit): the spring returns to its original length when unloaded, storing energy as elastic potential energy = ½·k·e² (the triangle's area). Plastic (beyond the limit): it's permanently deformed and won't fully spring back.
