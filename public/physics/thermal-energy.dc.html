Thermal energy

Pour energy into something and it usually gets hotter — but how much hotter depends on what it's made of and how much there is. And at a melting or boiling point, the temperature stops rising even as you keep heating. Explore both with the specific-heat sandbox and the heating curve.

SPECIFIC HEAT CAPACITY (c)
The energy needed to raise 1 kg of a substance by 1 °C. Water's is huge (4200 J/kg°C) — it soaks up loads of energy for a small temperature rise, which is why the sea warms slowly and water makes a good coolant. Metals have small c, so they heat up fast.
LATENT HEAT (the flat parts)
On the flat sections the energy goes into breaking bonds to change state — not into raising the temperature. That hidden ("latent") energy is E = m·L. It's why steam scalds so badly: it dumps all its latent heat back as it condenses on your skin.
