Moments & turning forces

A small child can balance a heavy adult on a seesaw — if they sit far enough out. That's a moment: a turning effect that depends on both the force AND how far from the pivot it acts. Balance the beam, then see why a long spanner loosens a stubborn bolt.

Same push, longer handle → bigger distance from the bolt → bigger moment. That's why a long spanner (or a door handle far from its hinge) turns things so much more easily.
THE PRINCIPLE OF MOMENTS
When something is balanced (in equilibrium), the total clockwise moment equals the total anticlockwise moment about the pivot. Levers and gears use this to trade force for distance: push a long lever a long way with a small force to shift a big load a short way.
Moment is measured in newton-metres (N·m). Distance is the PERPENDICULAR distance from the pivot to the line of the force.
