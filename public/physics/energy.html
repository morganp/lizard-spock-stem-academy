// L1 — WHERE ENERGY COMES FROM
Some energy sources will run out; others keep refilling. Non-renewables — coal, oil, gas, uranium — took millions of years to form and we're burning through them fast. Renewables — sunlight, wind, waves, flowing water, geothermal heat — top themselves up every day. Almost all of them trace back to one giant reactor: the Sun.

// L2 — THE EIGHT ENERGY STORES
Modern physics doesn't talk about "types" of energy so much as where it's stored. Energy sits in a store until something transfers it somewhere else — by heating, by a force moving, by an electric current, or by radiation (light and sound).

// L3 — CONSERVATION & EFFICIENCY
The law of conservation of energy is one of the deepest rules in all of science: energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred between stores. So why does everything run down? Because most transfers spill some energy as heat to the surroundings — energy that's now too spread out to be useful. Efficiency measures how much you actually got where you wanted it:
efficiency = (useful energy out ÷ total energy in) × 100%
Swap the filament bulb for an LED in the diagram above: the useful (light) band goes from a sliver to almost the whole thing. Same electricity in — far less wasted. That's why we changed every bulb in the country.
