// L1 — WHAT A MACHINE ACTUALLY DOES
A simple machine is any tool that changes a force — usually making a small effort do a big job. It never gives you energy for free. If a ramp halves the force you need, you have to push twice as far. That fair trade is called the golden rule of machines: force × distance stays the same.
// L1 — MECHANICAL ADVANTAGE
Mechanical advantage is just how many times the machine multiplies your effort. An MA of 4 means a 100 N pull lifts a 400 N load. For a lever, MA is the ratio of the two arms; for a ramp, it's how long the slope is compared to its height; for a pulley, it's how many rope sections hold the load.
