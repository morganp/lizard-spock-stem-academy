// FIG. 02 — TRUE RELATIVE SIZES (DIAMETER)
All eight planets drawn to the same scale. Jupiter could swallow 1,300 Earths — and the Sun could swallow over a million. The four rocky worlds are tiny specks next to the giants.
// MOONS
A moon is a world that orbits a planet. Earth has one; Mars has two tiny ones; Jupiter and Saturn each have dozens. Our Moon is the fifth-largest in the solar system and the only one humans have walked on.
// WHAT ABOUT PLUTO?
Pluto was called the 9th planet until 2006. It's now a dwarf planet — too small and it shares its orbit region with other icy worlds in the Kuiper Belt. There are at least five known dwarf planets.
