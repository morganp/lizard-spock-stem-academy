The sky keeps time

The Earth spins once a day — that's why the Sun rises and sets. The Moon takes about a month to circle us, and as it goes we see different slices of its sunlit side. Spin the Earth to make day and night, then walk the Moon around its orbit to build every phase.

// FIG. 03 — ECLIPSES: WHEN THEY LINE UP
Solar eclipse
The Moon passes between the Sun and Earth, casting a shadow on us. For a few minutes, day turns to twilight. Only happens at new moon.
Lunar eclipse
The Earth passes between the Sun and Moon, so our shadow falls on the Moon. It glows dim red — a "blood moon". Only at full moon.
