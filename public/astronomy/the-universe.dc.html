// THE BIG CLUE: REDSHIFT
In the 1920s Edwin Hubble found that almost every galaxy is moving away from us — and the further away it is, the faster it goes. Their light is stretched to redder colours (redshift), like a siren dropping in pitch as it speeds off.
Run that backwards and everything was once packed into one point. That's the evidence for the Big Bang. We even still detect its faint afterglow — the cosmic microwave background — coming from every direction in the sky.
// FIG. 03 — THREE SHAPES OF GALAXY (CLICK TO EXPLORE)
Spiral
A flat, spinning disc with curved arms of bright young stars and gas. Our Milky Way is a spiral.
e.g. Milky Way, Andromeda
Elliptical
A smooth, egg-shaped ball of mostly old red stars, with little gas left to make new ones. Often huge.
e.g. M87
Irregular
No neat shape — often small galaxies pulled about by their neighbours' gravity, busy making new stars.
e.g. Magellanic Clouds
