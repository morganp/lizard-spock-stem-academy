The further out a planet is, the slower it moves and the longer its path — so its year is much longer. Neptune crawls at 5 km/s and takes 165 Earth years to go round once. The Sun's gravity is weaker far away, so less speed is needed to stay in orbit.
The astronomical unit (AU). Distances in space are so huge that kilometres get silly. So astronomers measure in AU — the distance from the Earth to the Sun, about 150 million km. Neptune is 30 AU out. Light, the fastest thing there is, still takes over 4 hours to reach it.
