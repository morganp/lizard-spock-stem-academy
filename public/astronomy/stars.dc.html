// WHY SOME STARS LOOK BRIGHTER
A star can look bright for two reasons: it's genuinely giving out huge amounts of light, or it's simply closer to us. A dim nearby star can outshine a giant one far away — just like a nearby candle looks brighter than a distant floodlight.
// LIGHT FROM THE PAST
Stars are so far away we measure their distance in light-years — how far light travels in a year (about 9.5 trillion km). The nearest star beyond the Sun is 4.2 light-years away, so its light left before you were born. Look up and you're seeing the past.
