// ENERGY IN LIVING THINGS — LEVEL L3

Photosynthesis stores energy — respiration spends it

Plants trap light energy and lock it into glucose; every living cell then releases that energy again by respiration. Run the pondweed lab below — change the light, the carbon dioxide and the temperature, and watch which one is holding the rate back.

As light increases the rate climbs — until a different factor (CO₂ or temperature) runs out and the line flattens into a plateau. That flat part is where light is no longer the limiting factor. Lower the CO₂ slider and watch the plateau drop.
// FIG. 02 — PHOTOSYNTHESIS (STORES ENERGY · ENDOTHERMIC)
carbon dioxide + water →(light)→ glucose + oxygen
6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
Happens in chloroplasts, powered by light trapped by chlorophyll. Glucose is used for respiration, stored as starch, or built into cellulose and proteins.
// FIG. 03 — RESPIRATION (RELEASES ENERGY · EXOTHERMIC)
glucose + oxygen → carbon dioxide + water
C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O
Aerobic respiration happens in mitochondria in every living cell, all the time, releasing the energy stored in glucose for life processes.
