This really happened. In the 1800s, soot from British factories blackened tree trunks. Pale peppered moths that had always been well hidden now stood out and were eaten, while rare dark moths thrived. Within decades most peppered moths near industrial cities were dark — a change first explained by Charles Darwin and Alfred Russel Wallace.
