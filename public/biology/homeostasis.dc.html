// KEEPING THE INSIDE STEADY — LEVEL L3

Your body holds itself steady with negative feedback

Homeostasis keeps conditions inside you — temperature, water, blood sugar — within a narrow safe range, whatever the outside world does. Drag the weather from freezing to scorching and watch the body fight to hold 37 °C. Then switch the control off and see what happens.

// FIG. 03 — HORMONES CONTROL BLOOD SUGAR
BLOOD SUGAR TOO HIGH ▲
Pancreas releases insulin
Insulin tells the liver and muscles to take glucose out of the blood and store it as glycogen. Blood sugar falls back to normal.
BLOOD SUGAR TOO LOW ▼
Pancreas releases glucagon
Glucagon tells the liver to break glycogen back down into glucose and release it. Blood sugar rises back to normal.
Same negative-feedback idea as temperature: a change is detected and reversed. In Type 1 diabetes the pancreas can't make insulin, so it must be injected.
