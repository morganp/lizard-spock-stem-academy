// MAKING THE NEXT GENERATION — LEVEL L2

How living things make more living things

Nothing lives forever, so every species must make new members. Most plants and animals do it sexually — a male sex cell joins a female one, and the offspring are a fresh mix of both parents. Let's take a flower apart to see exactly how.

TWO PARENTS
Sexual reproduction
A male sex cell (gamete) joins a female one. In flowers that's pollen + ovule; in animals it's sperm + egg. Because two sets of genes mix, the offspring are all a little different — that variation helps a species survive change.
ONE PARENT
Asexual reproduction
One parent makes copies of itself with no gametes — a strawberry runner, a potato tuber, a dividing bacterium. It's fast and needs no mate, but every offspring is an identical clone, so there's no variation.
