// THINNER THAN AN APPLE SKIN
If the whole Earth were shrunk to the size of an apple, the crust we live on would be thinner than the apple's skin. Everything you've ever seen — every mountain, ocean and forest — sits on that skin.
HOW WE KNOW
Earthquakes send seismic waves through the planet. The waves bend, speed up, slow down and even stop at each boundary — some waves can't pass through the liquid outer core at all. By timing them all over the world, scientists mapped the layers without ever digging down.
