The rock cycle turns forever

No rock lasts forever. Over millions of years, mountains crumble into sand, sand is buried and squeezed into rock, rock is cooked and folded, and deep down some of it melts back into magma — which cools into fresh rock and starts again. // NOTE — THE CYCLE HAS SHORTCUTS
The loop above is the classic path, but rock can jump the queue. Any rock at the surface can be weathered into sediment; any buried rock can be cooked into metamorphic rock; and any rock dragged deep enough can melt into magma. There's no fixed order — just a web of processes powered by the Sun (weathering, erosion) and the Earth's inner heat (melting, pressure).
