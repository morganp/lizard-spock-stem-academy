# Project instructions — Lizard-Spock STEM Academy

## Scientific figure standards
All figures/diagrams across the site must follow proper scientific journal/notebook conventions:
- Every figure needs a **label and number** (e.g. "FIG. 01 — ...") — already the site's convention for section headers; make sure the diagram *itself* also carries a caption/legend, not just the section eyebrow above it.
- Every diagram element that isn't self-evident needs a **legend or inline label** — colors, shapes, and icons must be identified (what does orange mean, what does grey mean, which dot is which particle/ion/atom).
- Keep the labelling scientifically accurate and legible at the diagram's actual render size — don't shrink text below readability to fit more labels; simplify the diagram instead.
- Prefer axis labels + units on any graph (not just tick numbers).
- Don't conflate levels of abstraction in one figure (e.g. don't inject atomic substructure into a diagram whose subject is molecular/mixture-level behavior) — dedicate the right figure to the right level of detail, and cross-link between them instead.
